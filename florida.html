Florida, much like other states it has so much more to offer than what most experience. For us it is no different. Unfortunately, our trips to Florida have been centered on taking the family to theme parks. However, we hope to visit again and experience what else the state has to offer.
We've visited Florida 3 times to date, experienced Disney World 3 times and Universal Studios once. Initially, my wife did not have an urge to visit Universal; however, as my eldest daughter entered her early teens she became a Harry Potter fan. With that I was able to talk her into visiting and it honestly blew her mind. While the park is smaller, it is EXTREMELY generous for parents of young children. Let's say you have 2 older kids and one small child. Well small children can't ride many rides and the parents want to experience the rides as well. When you get to the front of the line, one parent will be guided to a small room on the side to care for the small child while the others can ride, when the ride is done they come get you, you swap out and BAM you're on the ride. No line waiting (you've already done it once right!). Disney gives you a quick pass and you have to go back around...after waiting for the first group to get off and (if you know, you know) quick passes still require a bit of wait. We will be going back to Universal and we can't wait to experience NINTENDO LAND.
